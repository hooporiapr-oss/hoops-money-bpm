03 / The Physical Brake Methodology

Isoinertial training, done right.

The physical-brake layer of the system is built on isoinertial flywheel training — a methodology that trains eccentric strength, the capacity to absorb and decelerate force, rather than just producing it. Strength programs that only train concentric force production build players who can jump but can't land. Isoinertial training builds the other half.

BPM Programs works with a dedicated isoinertial training director who has built the protocols that make the YoYo flywheel deployable in a basketball context — landing mechanics, deceleration loading, second-jump absorption, contact tolerance. The equipment without the methodology is just a flywheel. The methodology is what makes it the physical brake.

Programs engaging the full three-layer system get access to the methodology, the deployment protocols, and direct consultation on how to integrate isoinertial work into the strength-and-conditioning side of the program.
